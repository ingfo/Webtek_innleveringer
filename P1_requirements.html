23. september 2017

Client

Kari Øvernes, artist.

Øvernes is a retired dentist who now is a self employed-artist and mostly do acrylic paintings. She was an art student under Karin Hernø-Toftild, and others, at Kunstskolen Andenes in 2000-2001. Her paintings have been exhibited in Tvedestrand in 2003, Løddesøl in 2006 and at a studio exhibition in Nyborg in 2011.

Purpose

To make an online portfolio and contact page for Kari Øvernes.

The purpose of making a website for this client is to make an online portfolio which can also be used as a contact page for potential buyers. Kari Øvernes already has an existing blog which the website will be based on. The website will be a way for her to show off her works in the same sense as in a picture book, but online and with more interaction. Øvernes lives in a small community, so an online portfolio could expand her potential buyers as well.

Goals

The website intends to create an online portfolio for Kari Øvernes by the end of November 2017.

  • The page shall optimize the experience of viewing the different artworks, compared to the existing blog.
  • It is important that the artwork is in focus and that the rest of the site is more simple and easy to navigate.
  • The page interaction to be responsive to smaller devices like mobiles and tablets with touch screens.
  • The website shall be simple to update, for example if there are new paintings to add.

Requirements:

Functional

The webpage shall:

  • Display 29 of Kari Øvernes' pictures and information about the pictures.
  • Be able to filter out pictures by categories, size, price and material.
  • Have an all-pictures-view where the user can access the "slide view" by clicking a picture.
  • Have a possibility to move from one picture to another in a "slide view"
    • by using keyboard arrows
    • by clicking
  • Contain information about the artist, Kari Øvernes, and her contact information, and her exhibitions.

Non-functional

  • The page shall be intuitive, easy to navigate.
  • The fonts and overall page style shall be in harmony with Kari's art.
  • The page shall be easy to use for mobile and tablet users.
  • The size of the page content shall be adjusted to the window size.
  • The page shall load within a second.
  • The artworks shall be in focus on the page; information about the pictures shall not steal the artworks' attention.
  • The webpage shall be in Norwegian.

Target group

Potential buyers and people wanting to see the pictures. A Norwegian audience.

For the potential buyers every painting should be easy to view, and it should be easy to find information about the picture (prize, size, if the picture is sold or not). We also want to make it easy for the buyers to get in touch with the artist about purchasing a painting.

The other main target group are people clicking by wanting to look at her pictures. This group includes (but is not limited to): newer and more inexperienced painters wanting to see the works of a more trained painter, Øvernes' former teachers (using the pictures as an example of the work of one of their former students), and Øvernes' friends and family.

We focus on users with Norwegian as a main language, and because of that the content of the website will be written in Norwegian. This is a decision we have made in agreement with Øvernes, based on her preferences.

Content

Home

A simple homepage with a menu linking to the other pages, and showing some of Øvernes' pictures (as header-image/background).

Gallery

Main view: All the paintings will be displayed in the gallery, which will include filters for the different type of paintings and additional information about the paintings.

Slide-show: View displaying one image at a time, and information about the picture. Zoom-function.

Exhibitions

Containing information about (previous and future) exhibitions.

About

Information about Kari Øvernes, her life, why she paints etc.

Contact

Øvernes’ e-mail address and information about how buyers can get in touch.

Website hosting

The website will be hosted at a folk.ntnu.no
We will talk to the client whether she wants an own domain, and how she wants to manage the page after this semester.
